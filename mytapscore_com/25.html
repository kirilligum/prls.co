  • Can you explain the health risks associated with contaminants typically found in agricultural runoff in drinking water?

    Agricultural runoff often introduces various contaminants into drinking water, including pesticides, fertilizers (like nitrates), heavy metals, and pathogens. These can pose serious health risks such as gastrointestinal illnesses, reproductive issues, and developmental problems in children. Long-term exposure to nitrates, for example, can lead to methemoglobinemia, or 'blue baby syndrome,' which affects the blood's ability to carry oxygen. Additionally, pesticides have been linked to neurological disorders and certain cancers. Regular testing of drinking water is crucial to detect such contaminants.
